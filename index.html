understanding semantic HTML5

Published: January 1, 2024

What is Semantic HTML?

Semantic HTML uses HTML5 elements to define the meaning and structure of web content, rather than just its presentation. This makes your content more understandable to both humans and machines (like search engines and screen readers).

Benefits of Using Semantic HTML

  • Improved Accessibility: Screen readers can better understand and navigate your content.
  • Better SEO: Search engines can easily identify the key parts of your page, improving your search ranking.
  • Cleaner Code: Semantic HTML makes your code more readable and maintainable.
  • Increased Reusability: Well-structured content is easier to style and repurpose.

Examples of Semantic HTML5 Tags

Here are some common semantic HTML5 tags:

  • <header>: Represents the introductory content of a section or page.
  • <nav>: Represents a section of navigation links.
  • <main>: Represents the main content of the document.
  • <article>: Represents a self-contained composition in a document.
  • <section>: Represents a thematic grouping of content.
  • <aside>: Represents content that is tangentially related to the content around it.
  • <footer>: Represents the footer of a section or page.
